REAPATION

Sequence of Yoga Poses

Sukhasana (Easy Pose) with Gentle Stretch

  • Sit cross-legged on the mat.
  • Keep your spine straight and hands resting on your knees or in your lap.
  • Take a deep breath in and stretch your arms overhead.
  • Exhale and bring them back down. Repeat 3-5 times.

Cat-Cow Stretch (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

  • Start in a tabletop position (hands under shoulders, knees under hips).
  • Inhale, arch your back (cow pose), and look upward.
  • Exhale, round your back (cat pose), tucking your chin towards your chest.
  • Repeat 5-10 times to release tension in the spine.

  • Kneel on the mat and sit back on your heels.
  • Stretch your arms forward, resting your forehead on the mat.
  • Breathe deeply for 1-2 minutes.

Viparita Karani (Legs-Up-The-Wall Pose)

  • Lie on your back and elevate your legs up against a wall.
  • Rest your arms by your sides, palms facing up.
  • Stay here for 5-10 minutes to calm the nervous system.

Pranayama (Breathing Techniques)

  • Sit in a comfortable position.
  • Use your thumb to close the right nostril, inhale through the left.
  • Close the left nostril with your ring finger and exhale through the right.
  • Inhale through the right nostril, then switch and exhale through the left.
  • Repeat for 5-10 cycles.

Ujjayi (Ocean Breath)

  • Inhale deeply through your nose.
  • Exhale through your nose while slightly constricting your throat to create a "whispering" sound.
  • Continue for 5-10 minutes.

Meditation

  • Return to Sukhasana or any comfortable sitting position.
  • Keep your spine tall and your hands in Gyan Mudra (thumb and index finger touching).
  • Close your eyes and focus on your breath.
  • Notice the inhale and exhale without altering them.
  • Imagine a peaceful scene like a serene lake or a glowing light at your heart center.

Post Meditation

  • Lie down in Savasana (Corpse Pose) for 5 minutes to relax completely.
  • Gently return to seated position and open your eyes.
  • Practice daily, preferably in the morning or evening.
  • Stay consistent and patient, as meditation takes time to deepen.
