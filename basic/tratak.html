What is Tratak Dhyan?

Tratak Dhyan is a meditative technique where you fix your gaze on an external object (like a candle flame) or an internal image (visualized in the mind). This practice helps clear mental distractions and allows the practitioner to attain a heightened state of awareness.

Preparation

  • Practice in a distraction-free, dimly lit room
  • If using a candle, place it at eye level about 2-3 feet away from you.
  • Ensure the flame is steady and not flickering due to air movement
  • Sit in a cross-legged position (e.g., Sukhasana) or on a chair with a straight back.
  • Keep your head, neck, and spine aligned.
  • Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths to relax your body and mind.
  • Fix your gaze on the chosen object without blinking.
  • Fix your gaze on the chosen object without blinking.
  • Maintain an unwavering focus for as long as you can without blinking
  • If tears form in your eyes, allow them to flow naturally.
  • After gazing for a few minutes (or until your eyes feel strained), gently close your eyes.

  • Open your eyes slowly and return to normal awareness.
  • End the session with gratitude and reflection.
